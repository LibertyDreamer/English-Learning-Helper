Somebody and Anybody Quiz

Somebody: In the sentence "I’m gonna kill somebody," it implies that the speaker has a specific but unnamed target.

Anybody: In "I’m gonna kill anybody," the speaker means that they will kill whoever gets in their way – literally anyone.

Key Difference: "Somebody" refers to a specific, although unnamed, person, whereas "anybody" refers to any person without a specific target.

Movie villain example: "I’m gonna kill somebody!" shows he has a target in mind, while "I’m gonna kill anybody!" shows he’s out of control.
